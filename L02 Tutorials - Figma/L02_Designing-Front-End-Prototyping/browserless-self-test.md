# L02 Browserless Self-Test Report

**Exercise:** Test 2 websites using only mouse clicks — no browser Back, Forward, Refresh, address bar or keyboard shortcuts.

---

## Website 1: FairPrice Online
**URL:** https://www.fairprice.com.sg
**Task:** Find a Soy Milk product

### Steps taken (mouse clicks only)
1. Landed on FairPrice homepage.
2. Clicked the **Search icon** in the top navigation bar.
3. Typed "soy milk" using keyboard input (only navigation is browserless; search box entry is acceptable).
4. Clicked the **Search** button.
5. Results page loaded showing multiple soy milk products.
6. Clicked on **Marigold Soy Milk (Original)** product card to view product detail.

**Total navigation clicks:** 3 clicks (search icon → search button → product card)

### Observations
- The homepage uses a prominent top-navigation bar with category dropdowns, but the quickest route to a product was via the search icon rather than browsing category menus.
- The search results page clearly displayed product names, prices, and images in a grid layout, making it easy to identify the correct product at a glance.
- Without the browser address bar or Back button, I relied entirely on the site's own navigation links (logo to return home, breadcrumbs in the product detail page).
- The breadcrumb trail on the product page (Home > Beverages > Soy Milk) would allow backward navigation without the browser Back button.

**Ease of navigation:** Easy — the site provides clear in-page navigation paths.

---

## Website 2: Challenger Online
**URL:** https://www.challenger.sg
**Task:** Find a laptop product

### Steps taken (mouse clicks only)
1. Landed on Challenger homepage.
2. Clicked the **Computers & Laptops** category link in the top navigation menu.
3. A sub-menu appeared; clicked **Laptops** from the dropdown list.
4. The laptops listing page loaded with multiple laptop products.
5. Clicked on the first featured laptop card (ASUS VivoBook) to view product details.

**Total navigation clicks:** 3 clicks (Computers & Laptops category → Laptops sub-category → product card)

### Observations
- The top navigation mega-menu made it straightforward to drill directly into the Laptops category without needing search at all.
- The product listing page included filter options on the left sidebar (brand, price range, RAM), but these were not needed for this basic navigation task.
- Without the Back button, navigating from the product detail page back to the listing required clicking the breadcrumb (Home > Computers > Laptops) or using the site logo.
- The site logo click returned to the homepage, losing context — which is a usability gap that a redesign could address by making breadcrumbs more prominent.

**Ease of navigation:** Easy — category navigation is logically structured and requires few clicks.

---

## Summary

| Website | Task | Clicks to Product | In-site backward navigation available? |
|---|---|---|---|
| FairPrice Online | Find soy milk | 3 | Yes — breadcrumbs |
| Challenger Online | Find a laptop | 3 | Yes — breadcrumbs + category nav |

**Key takeaway:** Both websites require only 3 intentional clicks to reach a product from the homepage, which is considered good information architecture. Breadcrumb trails are the primary substitute for the browser Back button in both cases.
